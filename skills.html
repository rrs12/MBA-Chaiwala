WHY IS PRAFULL DIFFERENT???

INITIATIVE

When Praful was working at Mcdonald’s he used to take the initiative to talk to the customers to know about their views, etc. Also, when he started his startup the first day was not good but it was his initiative to interact with the customers the next day that resulted in his success. Not only this he has taken the initiative to provide a cohesive environment for networking at MBA Chaiwala and help society too!

SEEKING AND ACTING ON THE OPPORTUNITIES

Earlier Prafull was infatuated by the opportunities that IIM provided but by failing in CAT he did not stop there. He took it as an opportunity to do what he loves by believing in “Koi bhi kaam chota nahi hota.” In his words, he married to CHAI. (According to his JOSH Talk).

PERSISTENCE

The following picture says it all.

INFORMATION SEEKING

Be it during his work at McDonald’s or at MBA Chaiwala, he constantly believed in networking and talking to his clients and take reviews from them and understand customer psychology which he then used in the betterment of his business.

CONCERN FOR HIGH QUALITY OF WORK

Prafull always wanted to give high-quality chai to his clients and focused upon enriching the experience of his clients by creating the ambiance and the quality of chai the best. He served his chai in kulhad with toasts giving the notion of Ghar ki Chai. He also serves to corporate clients highlighting the fact that there must be something special about his chai.

SYSTEMATIC PLANNING

As I had mentioned before that Prafull believed in Dream Big, Think Smart, and Act Now, which portrays that he had his planning done both systematically as well as strategically to entice his customers.

SELF CONFIDENCE

Here I would just like to highlight one of the lines that Prafull spoke at Brut! which says everything about his self-confidence: “I used to support myself.” This one line so powerfully highlights the fact that Prafull was very much determined to achieve his dreams.

PERSUASION

Prafull had loads and tons of persuasion tactics in his pockets that he had learned through his experience during the preparation of CAT and GMAT. He used to persuade people to buy his chai by saying, “Hello sir/ma’am, would you like to try my chai? I have just started out today.”

USE OF INFLUENCE STRATEGIES

He had organized many events like free chai for single’s during Valentine’s day and chai for Kerala, and chai stalls at weddings and other events; on two basis: one, his chai, and two, his quirky Bollywood posters, games, and Caravan songs!
