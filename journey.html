THE JOURNEY TO ENTREPRENEURSHIP

Initial Struggle and Hurdles

Despite Prafull’s failure in cracking CAT, he decided to go forward with the idea and open his tea shop with a loan from his father of Rs. 8000. He obtained the funds from his father under the guise of taking a short-term international educational course. Then he thought about starting a cafe in Ahmedabad which would have required an investment of Rs. 15,00,000 which is not possible for a middle-class boy like him to wager his family’s saving. So, he decided to Dream Big, Think Smart, and Act Now. As a result of this, Prafull Billore launched his tea stand, MBA Chaiwala, in Ahmedabad on July 25, 2017.

The Beginning of MBA Chaiwala

The uneducated workers of the working class are believed to have roadside tea stands for a meager income. Praful was also scared to think of her father's annoyance when he learned that his son was running a tea stand instead of taking an MBA from a good school. With so many worries on her mind, Praful couldn't sleep for many nights. It took her 45 days to gather the courage to buy the containers and ingredients needed to make tea. Soon after, he set up the booth, but the journey was not easy. He did not even know how to make chai but he learned to do so to follow his passion!

Fortunately for Prafull Billore, MBA Chaiwala got off to a good start despite an uncertain start. His experience at McDonald's was worth it, as he was able to attract multiple customers to his booth, either by speaking to them in person or by speaking to them in English. Most of his clients were impressed with her fluency in English and appreciated her friendly conversations. But while his small business was on the rise, his family knew nothing of his new business.

In order to make MBA Chaiwala more attractive and attractive to the public, he served his tea in earthenware cups along with toast and handkerchief instead of using plastic cups. He sold the tea toast combo for just 30 rupees. On the first day with 0 sales, he felt depressed and thought that if customers didn't come to me, he had to interact with them, so on the second day, he sold 5 cups of chai and earned Rs. 150. In a month, he sold between 10,000 and 11,000 cups a day and his business grew slowly while his family did not realize his success. Later, his parents found his successful business on YouTube. Although his family did not like the idea of ​​setting up his tea stand, his family gradually accepted his new venture. So, as MBA Chaiwala began to gain a good client base, its competitors were angry with its success. Tea vendors in his area had been jealous of the growing popularity of the Chaiwala MBA. The tea vendors unanimously and brutally decided to expel MBA Chaiwala from their area.






The Beginning of MBA Chaiwala

Because of the relationship between Prafull and his clients, he received several calls and messages on social media inquiring about what occurred, where he was, and what his future intentions were. During the 12-15 days that his stall was closed, he missed brewing tea, serving tea to clients, counting notes, and meeting new people. After two weeks of staying at home, he returned to Ahmedabad in search of a new location for his tea stand. But this time he's in a better location where no one can hinder him in any manner.

Despite the setback, Praful Billore was still determined to make MBA Chaiwala a great success. With the help of some contacts, he approached a hospital owner and the relevant authorities and asked for a place for his tea stand. He obtained the permit and rented the room for 10,000 rupees and rebuilt his tea stall. This time, in addition to tea, she also offered sandwiches and coffee. Additionally, he built a good relationship with his clients and created a small space for a whiteboard in his store for job seekers to write their names, numbers, and qualifications so potential job providers can find and communicate with them.

Shortly after, Prafull Billore founded his restaurant called MBA Chaiwala. Although ridiculed for his lack of qualifications and occupation, he did not take the criticism personally. He even proudly stated that MBA at MBA Chaiwala does not mean Master of Business Administration, but rather `Mr. Billore Ahmedabad Chaiwala.’ Given the fact that people used to mock him by saying that even after doing an MBA he is selling tea!

In a few years, MBA Chaiwala became more and more popular and established many franchises and cafes throughout India. His fame and reputation helped Prafull Billore earn millions of rupees from his successful business. Over the years, MBA Chaiwala has had the honor of attending over 200 events including women's empowerment, entrepreneurship, LinkedIn programs, etc. He has also been invited to many wedding parties and political demonstrations to serve his famous tea.



In addition to focusing on growing the Chaiwala MBA and its franchises, Prafull Billore also dedicates his time to supporting social causes such as the economic advancement of disadvantaged people and the empowerment of women by organizing fundraising and campaigns for them. From time to time he is invited as a guest speaker at renowned institutions such as the IIM and Harvard, where he talks about his passion and success. Due to his good presence of mind, strategy, and dedication, MBA Chaiwala had sales of Rs. 3 crore in 2019-20 with a 300 sq. ft restaurant and 25-30 employees.
